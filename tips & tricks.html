Scope
Description It would seem that some people don't know what scope is. Scope is a popular brand of mouthwash formulated to help prevent cavities and freshen your breath. There's also the scope related to anchoring which is equally important to the type and size of anchor you're using. Scope is a term used when discussing the ratio of chain and rode used to water depth and freeboard height. Using chain only the scope can be much less than when using a short length of chain with rode, due to the added weight of chain versus line. The amount of scope will also be dependant on anchor size and weight, chain size, boat windage, weather conditions, bottom type, water depth and achorage space but it's always better to have more scope rather than less, especially in shallow water with a big surge or in gusty and squally conditions. A minimum scope in deep water and calm conditions would be 3 to 1 whereas in shallow water with a big surge or waves you may want to go as high as 10 to 1. In your average sandy anchorage under average conditions a 5 or 6 to 1 scope should be adequate.
